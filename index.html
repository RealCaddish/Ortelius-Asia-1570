Map of Ortelius' Asia

I wanted to make this map as an opportunity in search of historic events in 1570 when it was orginally commissioned and published by Gilles Coppens de Diest at Antwer during the prescribed Golden Age of Netherlandish Cartography. We can see that many natural phenomenon provided physical borders among different polities in the region. Additionally, Ortelius' map retains authority when place names provided a traditional means of claiming territory.

As Monmonier writes, " Despite cartography's obvious role in settling territorial disputes, maps of past boundaries and obsolete place names are tempting fodder for anyone eager to resurrect old land claims or demand reparations"

Asiae Nova Descripto is a prime example of how the authority of the map is tied to place names in time and space. We can see that many names from this map are recognizable even today.
